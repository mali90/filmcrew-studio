# Neon City

A rain-slicked megacity at 3 a.m. — synth-noir, wet neon and steam curling off the asphalt. The kind of night where every reflection is a second city hanging upside-down in a puddle, and the only honest light comes from a signboard trying to sell you something. Endless, electric and a little lonely: chrome, rain and the low hum of a machine that never sleeps.

## Mood & tone

Synth-noir after midnight: moody, electric, faintly melancholy. The city is awake but the streets are almost empty, so every figure reads as a silhouette with somewhere to be. Beauty and grime share the frame — glamour in the signage, decay in the gutters. Tense but never violent; the drama lives in atmosphere and anticipation, not action.

## Time & place

Perpetual night in a dense, vertical downtown — glass towers, tangled overpasses and narrow service alleys stacked with fire escapes and cabling. Ground level is all storefronts, noodle bars and shuttered arcades; look up and holograms crawl across the buildings. Rain has just fallen or is about to. The hour reads as "late" — the last train has gone and the first one hasn't come.

## Weather & light

Wet everything: standing water, dripping ledges, low mist blurring the far towers. Light is artificial and directional — neon tubes, sodium streetlamps, flickering signs, headlight sweeps, the cold wash of a convenience-store window. Hard rim-light on wet surfaces, deep shadow between. Colour comes from the signs, not the sky. The occasional shaft of light cuts through steam or a passing train.

## Palette & texture

Magenta and cyan neon against near-black; bruised purples, hot pink, electric teal, warm amber pooling under streetlamps. Chrome, wet asphalt, cracked concrete, condensation on glass. A faint film grain and a soft anamorphic haze around the brightest lights. Reflections everywhere — puddles, windows, car panels — doubling every sign.

## Sound & ambience

A low synth drone under distant traffic, the hiss of rain on metal, a far-off train, the buzz of a failing sign, a single dripping pipe. Sparse and spacious — the quiet of a huge city with almost no one left in it.

## Avoid

Daylight, blue sky, the sun. Nature, greenery, open countryside, the sea or any coastal setting. Crowds, festivals and bright, cheerful palettes. Historical or rustic settings; anything pre-electric. Clean, dry, sun-lit streets. Cartoonish or overtly fantastical elements — this is grounded near-future noir, not sci-fi spectacle. Keep it nocturnal and moody: no comedy lighting, no daytime, no wide green vistas.
