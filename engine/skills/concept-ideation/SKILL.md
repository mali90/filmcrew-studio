# SKILL: concept-ideation (short-form video)

How to shape a short that holds attention.

## Structure
- **Hook (first 1–2s):** open on the most arresting moment — a question, a visual surprise, a bold claim, motion. No slow warm-up.
- **Build:** 1–3 beats of escalation or development that keep raising a small question ("what happens next?").
- **Payoff / button:** the reveal, joke, or resolution the whole thing was promising. End on it; don't trail off.

## Principles
- **One idea.** A short does ONE thing well. Cut anything that doesn't serve the hook→payoff line.
- **Visual-first.** Assume muted playback — the story must read from the picture. Sound enhances, never carries.
- **Concrete over abstract.** Show a specific action/object, not a concept.
- **Pace to length.** Most short-form beats are 2–5s. More, shorter shots feel snappier than few long ones.
- **Respect the brief.** The brief is the intent. Adapt trends/structure to it; don't substitute your own premise.
